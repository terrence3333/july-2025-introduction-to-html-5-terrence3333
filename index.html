Introduction

This page demonstrates how to use HTML5 semantic elements effectively while also keeping accessibility and SEO in mind. Good semantic markup ensures better user experience and improved search engine visibility.

Understanding Accessibility

Accessibility ensures that websites are usable by everyone, including people with disabilities. Use of semantic HTML helps screen readers understand the content. For example, headings must follow a logical order, and navigation should be properly labeled.

SEO Basics

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is the practice of improving a webpage’s visibility in search engines. Using semantic tags like <header>, <main>, and <section>, and writing descriptive content with keywords, helps crawlers understand your page better.

Conclusion

Building a web page with HTML5 isn’t just about structure—it’s also about being inclusive and visible. With proper semantic elements, accessibility features, and basic SEO, your site becomes more user-friendly and discoverable.
